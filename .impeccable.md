## Design Context

### Users
German KMU (small/medium business) owners and decision-makers: Handwerker, Gastronomen, Immobilienmakler, Dienstleister. Age 35–60, not necessarily tech-savvy. They visit the site to evaluate whether to trust Clavion with their business processes. Primary device: desktop (business hours), mobile (on the go). Context: evaluating a purchase decision, skeptical, time-pressed.

### Brand Personality
- **3 words**: Precise · Trustworthy · Energetic
- Voice: Direct, no marketing fluff. German B2B tone — credible, confident, no hype.
- Emotional goal: Instill confidence that automation actually works and is safe (DSGVO-konform).
- Anti-reference: Generic SaaS landing pages with pastel gradients and stock illustrations.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Dark theme: #0a0a0a background, cyan #00E5FF accent
- Fonts: Syne (display/headings) + Inter (body) — NOTE: both are in the reflex-reject list, but are already deeply embedded in the existing production site
- Glassmorphism cards with cyan borders — intentionally used as the design system (glass-card utility)
- Animated demos and flow diagrams as key differentiators
- Anti-pattern note: The cyan-on-dark + glassmorphism combination is a flagged AI aesthetic, but it is the established brand identity at this point — improvements should work within it, not against it

### Design Principles
1. **Show, don't tell** — animated demos replace static screenshots; trust is earned by demonstration
2. **Conversion first** — every section has a CTA path; navigation and CTAs are always visible
3. **DSGVO-trust signals** — compliance and German market specificity must be prominent
4. **Technical credibility** — the design should feel like it was made by people who understand technology
5. **Mobile parity** — mobile users get full functionality, not a stripped-down version
